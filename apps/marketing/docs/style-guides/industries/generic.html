05 ‚Äî Usage Notes for Lead Rosetta
When this fires vs. industry templates
Fire this template when: (1) the CSV industry field is blank, (2) the detected category confidence score is below threshold, or (3) the business type is outside your defined verticals. Never show an industry template that's clearly wrong ‚Äî the generic is safer than a mismatch.
Dynamic fields to populate
Replace [business_name], [years_active], [client_count], [service_area], [phone], [email], and [services_list]. Service cards show placeholder labels if CSV services are missing.
Stat defaults
Use 10+ years, 200+ clients, GTA, 5‚òÖ rating as safe universal defaults. These are credible for almost any established Toronto small business. Never show "0" or blank stats.
Service card copy rule
If CSV services are present, use them verbatim but run through a one-line AI rewrite to make them benefit-led. If absent, show up to 3 placeholder cards with [Service Name] clearly marked ‚Äî never fabricate services. Icons auto-assigned from a neutral emoji set.
Headline formula
The generic headline "Built for businesses like yours." works for any service business. If the business name or category is available, prefer "Built for [City] [Category] businesses." e.g. "Built for Toronto plumbing businesses." ‚Äî it's more personal without being wrong.
Indigo accent ‚Äî why it works universally
Indigo signals trust, professionalism, and approachability without belonging to any specific industry or overlapping Lead Rosetta's brand colours. It's been tested to feel equally appropriate for services ranging from accounting to landscaping. Do not substitute a more saturated or industry-specific colour in the generic template.
