05 — Usage Notes for Lead Rosetta
Dynamic fields to populate
Replace [studio_name], [services_list], [location], [booking_url], [email]. The marquee strip auto-populates with service categories pulled from the CSV services field.
Service menu defaults
Show max 4–5 services with placeholder pricing using from $XX. If no pricing in CSV, use category-standard defaults: hair from $75, nails from $40, facials from $90. Always say "from" — never a fixed price.
The marquee is non-negotiable
The animated scrolling strip is the highest-engagement element in this template. It signals life and momentum. Always include it. Populate with: season + city + top 3 service categories + "By Appointment."
Button style rule
Buttons are deliberately square-cornered (no border-radius). This is intentional — it gives the template an editorial, fashion-forward feel. Do not add rounded corners. It would break the aesthetic entirely.
Light vs dark hero
The hero is always espresso dark on the left, cream pale on the right. Never swap or make both light. The contrast is the design. It signals luxury and editorial confidence — exactly what salon owners want to project.
Tone rule
Copy should feel calm, considered, and subtly aspirational. Avoid words like "affordable," "deal," "discount," "cheap." Prefer "curated," "ritual," "considered," "boutique." The demographic responds to quality language, not value language.
Email: include a snippet or image of the site
When sending the demo link via email, include a preview image or snippet of the generated website (e.g. a screenshot or link-preview card). Recipients are more likely to open and click when they can see what the site looks like in the email.
